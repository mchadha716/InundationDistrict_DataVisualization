Project G01: Rising Sea Levels, DS 4200 S22

Mira Chadha, Michelle Lo, Annetta Stogniew

Service-Learning Course Project as part of Ds 4200 S22 Information Presentation & Visualization, taught by Prof. Cody Dunne, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

This project will consist of data visualizations about rising sea levels. The graphics will show how flooding is increasing, how sea levels are rising, and how vulnerable various buildings will be to sea levels over time. This project is beneficial to understanding more about climate change and how it can affect coastal cities such as Boston. The resulting data will be used in an upcoming documentary film about how rising sea levels will affect Boston, particularly its Seaport neighborhood.

Rising sea levels are being caused by rapidly melting ice caps and rising global average temperatures. As climate change stands now, these sea levels will “wreak havoc on coastal cities across the world.” Some cities listed by a United Nations report include Mumbai, New York, Shanghai, and Miami. Boston, a city that already experiences regular flooding, is predicted to face up to 1.5 feet of sea-level rise by 2050. This would primarily affect buildings in the Seaport district. Data visualizations that show how flooding is increasing, how sea levels are rising, and how vulnerable buildings will be to rising sea levels will increase the Boston community’s awareness of the problem as well as motivate further research on the effect of rising sea levels in Boston. These data visualizations are planned to appear in an upcoming documentary film.
